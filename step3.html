POLLINATION

Collection of pollen

When a bee for exemple collects nectar and pollen from the flower of a plant, some pollen from the stamens (the male reproductive organ of the flower) sticks to the hairs of her body.

Fertilization

When she visits the next flower, some of this pollen is rubbed off onto the stigma, or tip of the pistil (the female reproductive organ of the flower.) When this happens, fertilization is possible, and a fruit, carrying seeds, can develop.

Effect of bees on fruits

The most important thing that bees do is pollinate. Flowers that are visited more often by bees will produce larger and more uniform fruit than those visited less often. Pollination of fruits trees is required to produce seeds with surrounding fruit. This beneficial effect of pollination is most obvious in tree fruit.
